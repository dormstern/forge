## 3. Wedge

The one thing you do that nobody else does. Not a feature — a workflow shift.

[YOUR ANSWER HERE]

[EXAMPLE: Auto-correlated incident timeline that pulls from any 3+ observability tools without writing parsers.]

## 4. Validation cheap-tests

Three things you can do this week to test the thesis. Not "build an MVP." Cheap experiments — interviews, landing-page tests, a manual prototype.

1. [TEST 1]
2. [TEST 2]
3. [TEST 3]

[EXAMPLE: (1) Text 5 SOC managers and ask "what's the worst part of triaging a Sev-2?" (2) Spin up a landing page with a "join the beta" button, run $200 of LinkedIn ads at SOC managers. (3) Manually correlate one real incident timeline for an existing user, charge $200 for the report.]

confidence: 1/5

## 5. Competition

Top 3 alternatives, in one sentence each. Include "do nothing" if relevant.

1. [COMPETITOR 1]
2. [COMPETITOR 2]
3. [COMPETITOR 3 OR "do nothing"]

[EXAMPLE: (1) PagerDuty Incident Workflows — strong incident response, weak on cross-tool correlation. (2) Splunk Mission Control — owned by buyers already on Splunk, expensive and slow. (3) Do nothing — most teams just live with the pain because no one has time to evaluate yet another tool.]

confidence: 1/5

## 6. Pricing hypothesis

Rough WTP guess. Not what you want to charge — what evidence suggests they'd actually pay.

[YOUR ANSWER HERE]

[EXAMPLE: $500–$2k/month per SOC team, based on what they currently pay for adjacent tools and the dollar value of analyst hours saved.]

confidence: 1/5
